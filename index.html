Section 1: Introduction to FM-PCT

This study introduced a novel thermal-wave Fourier-spectrum-based tomographic modality, FM-PCT, as a derivative of TC-PCT and pulse thermography. Employing matched filtering and frequency-domain delay combination, FM-PCT processes the stacks of original IR camera frame thermal sequences in the frequency spectrum of the excitation time-domain waveform, thereby generating three-dimensional images of material internal structures.

Section 2: Tomograms

Quantitative detection of subsurface defects is a hot issue in infrared thermography and photothermal tomography. Conventional infrared thermography only provides 2D images and analysis for defect detection. In addition, advanced photothermal tomography like enhanced truncated-correlation photothermal coherence tomography significantly relies on the frame rate and has a small imaging area. To overcome foregoing issues, we explored a novel photothermal tomography technique FM-PCT, which can be used for large specimen with high depth-resolved capability even under a low frame rate (50 Hz).

Low-velocity impact damage evaluation is important for non-destructive testing (NDT) techniques and mechanics analysis. General NDT techniques like ultrasound, infrared, X-ray radiation provide limited 2D information. Even if using a X-ray computed tomography (CT), limited by sample-size, CT exhibits low axial resolution. Micro-CT can effectively solve this problem. However, it is too expensive thus not suitable for the industrial application. Now, FM-PCT is employed for impact damage evaluation and provides promising results.
